How to Use Chase Credit Card Points for Free Travel

Traveling the world is a dream for many, but rising costs and economic uncertainties can make it seem out of reach. Fortunately, Chase Ultimate Rewards® points offer a powerful way to unlock free travel—even in today’s volatile economy. Whether you're escaping inflation at home or exploring sustainable destinations, mastering Chase points can turn your wanderlust into reality.

Why Chase Points Are a Game-Changer

Chase Ultimate Rewards® is one of the most flexible rewards programs, offering multiple ways to redeem points for flights, hotels, and unique experiences. With strategic planning, you can stretch your points further, especially as travel demand surges post-pandemic.

The Best Chase Cards for Travel

Not all Chase cards are created equal. Here are the top options for maximizing travel rewards:

  • Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card: A great starter card with a low annual fee and strong rewards.
  • Chase Sapphire Reserve®: Premium perks like airport lounge access and higher point values.
  • Chase Freedom Unlimited®: Earns cash back, but points can be transferred to Sapphire cards for travel redemptions.

Maximizing Your Points for Free Flights

1. Transfer to Airline Partners

Chase partners with 14 airlines, including United, Southwest, and international carriers like Singapore Airlines. Transferring points can yield incredible value—sometimes up to 2 cents per point or more.

Pro Tip: Look for "sweet spots" like:
- United MileagePlus: Book round-trip flights to Europe for just 60,000 points.
- British Airways Avios: Short-haul flights in the U.S. can cost as little as 7,500 points.

2. Book Through the Chase Travel Portal

If you don’t want to deal with airline transfers, the Chase Travel Portal lets you book flights directly. Points are worth:
- 1.25 cents each with the Sapphire Preferred.
- 1.5 cents each with the Sapphire Reserve.

Hot Deal: During off-peak seasons, you might snag a round-trip flight to Mexico for under 20,000 points.

Luxury Stays Without the Price Tag

1. Transfer to Hotel Partners

Chase partners with Hyatt, IHG, and Marriott. Hyatt offers the best value, with luxury stays starting at just 12,000 points per night.

Example: The Park Hyatt Paris-Vendôme typically costs $1,000+ per night but can be booked for 30,000 points.

2. Use Points for Unique Experiences

Beyond flights and hotels, Chase lets you book:
- Airbnb-style stays via the travel portal.
- VIP concert tickets or culinary experiences.

Navigating Current Travel Challenges

Inflation-Proof Travel

With prices soaring, points act as a financial shield. A $1,500 flight might only cost 100,000 points, saving you real cash for other expenses.

Sustainable Travel Options

Many travelers now prioritize eco-friendly trips. Chase partners with airlines like JetBlue, which offers carbon-neutral flights, and hotels with strong sustainability practices.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Letting points expire (they don’t, but devaluations happen).
  • Not comparing redemption options—always check partner airlines vs. the portal.
  • Overlooking transfer bonuses (Chase occasionally offers 20-30% bonus points for certain airlines).

Final Pro Tips

  • Stack points by using multiple Chase cards.
  • Time your redemptions—avoid peak travel dates for better availability.
  • Follow travel blogs for limited-time transfer deals.

With Chase points, the world truly is your oyster—even in uncertain times. Start planning your next adventure today!
