Dillard's Credit Card Limit Increase: How to Request One

In today’s fast-paced economy, managing credit wisely is more important than ever. With inflation, rising costs of living, and economic uncertainty, having access to additional credit can provide much-needed financial flexibility. If you’re a Dillard’s credit cardholder, you may be wondering how to request a credit limit increase to better align with your spending needs. This guide will walk you through the process, the benefits, and key considerations to keep in mind.

Why Request a Dillard’s Credit Card Limit Increase?

A higher credit limit isn’t just about spending more—it’s about financial empowerment. Here’s why you might consider requesting an increase:

1. Improved Credit Utilization Ratio

Your credit utilization ratio (the percentage of available credit you’re using) plays a major role in your credit score. A higher limit can lower this ratio, potentially boosting your score.

2. Greater Purchasing Power

Dillard’s is a premier retailer for fashion, home goods, and beauty products. A higher limit means more flexibility during sales, holidays, or emergencies.

3. Enhanced Financial Security

In uncertain economic times, having extra available credit can serve as a backup for unexpected expenses.

4. Exclusive Perks

Some Dillard’s cardholders enjoy special rewards, discounts, and financing offers. A higher limit may unlock additional benefits.

How to Request a Dillard’s Credit Limit Increase

Requesting a credit limit increase with Dillard’s is straightforward, but preparation is key. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Check Your Credit Score

Before applying, review your credit report. A strong score (typically 670 or higher) increases approval odds. You can check your score for free through services like Credit Karma or AnnualCreditReport.com.

Step 2: Gather Necessary Information

Dillard’s may ask for:
- Current income
- Employment status
- Monthly housing payment
- Other financial obligations

Step 3: Choose Your Request Method

You can request an increase through:
- Online: Log in to your Dillard’s credit card account and look for the "Credit Limit Increase" option.
- Phone: Call customer service at the number on the back of your card.
- In-Store: Visit a Dillard’s location and speak with a representative.

Step 4: Submit Your Request

Be honest and concise when providing financial details. If denied, ask for reasons and work on improving those areas before reapplying.

Factors That Affect Approval

Dillard’s, like most issuers, considers several factors:

1. Payment History

Consistent on-time payments improve your chances. Late payments or defaults hurt them.

2. Income and Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

A higher income and lower DTI signal financial stability.

3. Credit History Length

Longer credit histories are viewed more favorably.

4. Recent Credit Inquiries

Too many hard inquiries in a short period can be a red flag.

What to Do If Your Request Is Denied

Don’t panic—denials happen. Here’s how to respond:

1. Review the Reason

The issuer must provide a denial reason under U.S. law. Common reasons include:
- Low credit score
- High existing debt
- Insufficient income

2. Improve Your Credit Profile

  • Pay down balances
  • Avoid new credit applications
  • Dispute errors on your report

3. Wait and Reapply

Issuers often recommend waiting 6-12 months before reapplying.

Alternatives to a Credit Limit Increase

If you’re not approved, consider:

1. Asking for a Soft Pull Review

Some issuers may reconsider without a hard inquiry.

2. Applying for a New Card

Another Dillard’s card or a general rewards card could provide additional credit.

3. Using a Secured Card

If rebuilding credit, a secured card can help improve your profile over time.

Final Tips for Success

  • Time your request wisely – After a raise or paying off debt is ideal.
  • Be polite and professional – Customer service reps can sometimes advocate for you.
  • Monitor your credit – Regular checks help you stay informed.

By following these steps, you’ll be in a strong position to secure a Dillard’s credit limit increase and enjoy greater financial freedom.
