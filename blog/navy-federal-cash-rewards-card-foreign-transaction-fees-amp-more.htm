Decoding the Fine Print: The Truth About Foreign Transaction Fees

So, what exactly is this fee that causes so much consternation? A foreign transaction fee is a charge levied by many credit card issuers, typically ranging from 1% to 3% of the purchase amount, on any transaction that either passes through a foreign bank or is denominated in a currency other than U.S. dollars. It's essentially a tax on your global citizenship. For frequent travelers or international shoppers, these fees can add hundreds of dollars to their annual expenses, silently eroding the value of any rewards they might be earning.

This policy aligns the card with the best travel-focused cards on the market and places it miles ahead of many standard cash-back cards, which often include these fees as a hidden revenue stream.
